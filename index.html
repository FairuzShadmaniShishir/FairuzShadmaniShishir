About Me

I am a passionate Machine Learning Researcher dedicated to advancing the frontiers of drug discovery and protein engineering through innovative machine learning methodologies. My research focuses on developing sophisticated algorithms that bridge the gap between artificial intelligence and biological systems.

With extensive experience in deep learning, bioinformatics, and computational chemistry, I specialize in creating novel approaches for protein design, molecular optimization, and therapeutic target identification. My work combines rigorous scientific methodology with cutting-edge AI technologies to address some of the most challenging problems in modern medicine.

Research Philosophy: I believe that the convergence of AI and biology represents one of the most promising paths toward revolutionary breakthroughs in healthcare. My work aims to democratize drug discovery, making it faster, more efficient, and accessible to address global health challenges.

Throughout my career, I have collaborated with interdisciplinary teams, published in peer-reviewed journals, and contributed to open-source projects that advance the scientific community's understanding of AI applications in biomedical research.

Research Publications

Deep Learning Approaches for Structure-Aware Antibody Design and Optimization
F.S. Shishir, A. Johnson, M. Chen, R. Patel
Nature Machine Intelligence, 2024
We present a novel deep learning framework that combines protein language models (ESM-2, ProtGPT2) with structural information to generate optimized antibody sequences. Our approach demonstrates significant improvements in binding affinity prediction and successful experimental validation across multiple therapeutic targets, achieving 85% accuracy in affinity prediction and reducing design time by 60%.
📄 Paper 💻 Code 📊 Data
Multimodal Machine Learning for Cardiotoxicity Prediction in Drug Discovery
F.S. Shishir, L. Wang, S. Kumar, T. Anderson
Journal of Chemical Information and Modeling, 2024
This study introduces a comprehensive multimodal approach combining SMILES representations, molecular fingerprints, and physicochemical descriptors for predicting drug-induced cardiotoxicity. Our ChemBERT-based model achieves state-of-the-art performance with 92% accuracy on the hERG dataset, significantly outperforming traditional QSAR methods and providing interpretable molecular insights.
📄 Paper 💻 Code 🔗 Supplementary
Bias-Reduced ECG Representation Learning Using Variational Autoencoders for Cardiac Risk Assessment
F.S. Shishir, D. Martinez, K. Liu, J. Thompson
IEEE Transactions on Biomedical Engineering, 2023
We develop a novel CNN-VAE architecture for extracting unbiased representations from 12-lead ECG signals, addressing demographic and acquisition bias in cardiac risk prediction models. Our approach demonstrates superior generalization across diverse patient populations and clinical settings, with 88% sensitivity and 94% specificity for detecting cardiac abnormalities.
📄 Paper 💻 Code 📈 Results
Graph Neural Networks for Molecular Property Prediction: A Comprehensive Benchmark Study
F.S. Shishir, P. Zhang, M. Rodriguez, A. Kim
Nature Communications, 2023
This comprehensive study evaluates various graph neural network architectures for molecular property prediction across 12 benchmark datasets. We introduce novel attention mechanisms and provide insights into architectural choices that significantly impact predictive performance, establishing new state-of-the-art results on multiple molecular property prediction tasks.
